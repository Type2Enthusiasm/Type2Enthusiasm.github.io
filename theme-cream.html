I’m drawn to hard problems where science, markets, and execution intersect.

I’m a PhD chemist with experience across scientific research, technology commercialization, startups, and early-stage investing. I’m interested in how complex scientific ideas become useful tools, products, companies, and investment opportunities.

My research has spanned stem cell differentiation, intrinsically disordered proteins, liquid-liquid phase separation, and protein aggregation. I’ve always been drawn to strange biological phenomena that sit at the edge of explanation, especially when they open new ways to understand, measure, or build. Along the way, I’ve used wet-lab experiments, spectroscopy, and computational tools to answer questions about biology and physics.
